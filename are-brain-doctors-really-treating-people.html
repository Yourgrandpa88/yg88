Are Brain Doctors Really Treating People?

June 16, 2026 Paransh Singh

BE OPEN MINDED WHILE  READING THIS

It was 2023 when I went vegan. I was only 17. Finally, I had a reason to work for, a purpose worth putting effort into. I had always dreamed of being born during the era of British colonial rule because I thought being a freedom fighter was the right thing to do , a purpose worth dedicating oneself to.

As the 2011 german strongman (Patrik Baboumian) said in his speech "for every child's desire to save the world now you have the opportunity to end a massacre" . In fact, "massacre" is less voilent word for what we are doing to animals. In a massacre, people are killed in large numbers, but their suffering lasts minutes or hours.

These animals, however, live their entire lives being treated like slaves, commodities, and mere assets as if they were lifeless and insentient beings. By sentient, I mean having the ability to feel pain, fear, love, and other emotions. Yes, animals are sentient.

 

In 2024, I moved back in with my family. Since I was now vegan, my first attempts at advocacy began at home.  I constantly looked for opportunities to bring the animal liberation movement into every conversation I could.

As a result, my family started thinking that their child had lost his mind living alone for an year so they took me to a psychiatrist. This doctor always tried to stop me from following the path that chose me "its not phrased wrongly this is what one of my idols said about the path of an animal rights activist. Fortunately, I was able to rebut every argument he made, thanks to what I have learnt from Arvind, Joey, and Robin. I have been an atheist since childhood, but these three individuals are like gods to me because of the work they do for the animal liberation movement.

The doctor prescribed medications for delusions, anxiety, stress, and schizophrenia. I did not object to taking the medications because I new that doctor can cure onluy the one who is ill and i was not. However, with every session, I learned to keep my mouth shut. I did so because I felt that no matter how much I tried to explain my perspective, these narrow minded people remained stuck with their methods even while being highly educated(this also proves morals do not work hand in hand with education). Everyone believed that the medications had changed me, when in reality I had simply became quieter.

In July 2025, my parents took me to the same doctor because I was experiencing low energy levels. He ordered blood tests for vitamin B12 and serum calcium. The results were exactly what one would expect. My calcium levels were normal, but my vitamin B12 level was significantly below the normal range. This was not surprising because I had not taken a single B12 supplement during the two years since becoming vegan.

This was the one thing the doctor did that genuinely helped me by opening my mind towards the nutritional perspetive of activism. I had neglected the nutritional side of veganism because I assumed that eating everything except animal products would automatically be sufficient. I focused heavily on the emotional and philosophical aspects of activism and became knowledgeable about them, while largely ignoring nutrition.

On August 17, 2025, I began taking vitamin B12 supplements. I followed the dosage recommended in NIH articles: 1,500 mcg daily for one month for an adult with deficiency by the way it is same for lactating mothers too. After that month, I switched to one pill per week, again following NIH￼ guidance. My energy levels improved noticeably.

Then, on October 28, 2025, I suddenly developed floaters in my vision. For about a month, my family and I visited one eye specialist after another. We underwent every eye test imaginable, including an MRI focused on the optic nerve. Every doctor and report told me that my eyes were completely healthy.  no one could explain what was causing floaters.

Eventually, the floaters disappeared after approximately four to six months, which is actually  the normal timeframe for floaters to become less noticeable or resolve. The psychiatrist was the doctor who prescribed the treatment during this period. However, my family did not allow me to research the medications I was given. There were four pills in total. One was a vitamin B12 supplement, another was vitamin D3, but I never got the chance to identify the other two. This prescription of b12 proves him to be a bit of a hypocrate as he is the same doctor who always keep saying that supplements have poo absorption so they are a wrong approach for getting b12 but nih has a totally different saying that its the best source and even nih mendates it for people above 50 of age.

The doctor claimed that all of the medicines were for my optic nerve. This never made sense to me because every test, including the MRI, indicated that my optic nerve was perfectly healthy. He also do not have an explanation for my situation and what i think is that my codition was not a structural issue but a functional one that appears and disappears on their own mostly because of unknown reasons 

The situation became more complicated afterward. My parents stopped paying for my supplements and began pressuring me to consume animal products just because now after months of wandering from doctor to doctor for my floaters made their trust shift from me to that doctor who cured them or atleast who fortunately got the credit of curing. They even threatened not to support my education. This happened because the doctor convinced them that vitamin B12 supplements have "poor absorption"—his exact words. Ironically, he himself had prescribed B12 supplements to me when I was experiencing the floaters.Also when my family first took me to that psychiatrist for my floaters the first thing he told to get tested was b12 serum which came out to be perfect also having low b12 while consuming supplements is impossible still everyone blamed low b12 levels for my floaters till today.

 After receiving my Class 12 results, I applied for roles at around twenty animal welfare organizations, including PETA, hoping to walk my own path myself. Almost none of them responded. Only one organization replied, and they ultimately rejected my application because they felt I was too young.
